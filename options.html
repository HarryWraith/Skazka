Subclasses in Skazka

In the worlds beyond Skazka, classes are rigid paths — the martial discipline of the fighter, the holy oaths of the paladin, the timeless teachings of druid and wizard. But in Skazka, where all planes converge and shadows bleed into waking life, no path is so narrow.

The crossroads twists destinies. Warriors are branded by planar scars, priests whisper vows to nameless gods, rogues step between veils, and sorcerers wake each morning with a stranger’s voice in their skull. The magic of Skazka does not merely grant power — it reshapes the soul, bending tradition into something new, something dangerous.

A Skazkan subclass is not a mere specialization. It is a mark upon the spirit, carved by gods long dead, forests that think, or dimensions that hunger. To embrace one is to accept a destiny outside the ordered rules of other realms.

Those who walk these crooked paths find themselves rewoven into the world’s tapestry:

  • Paladins guard the gates between planes, not just the temples of men.
  • Druids root themselves in whispering woods where the trees themselves judge intruders.
  • Rogues vanish into twilight rifts that no lantern can pierce.
  • Sorcerers and warlocks bargain not only with demons and dragons, but with the echoes of forgotten ages.

To take a subclass in Skazka is to declare allegiance to the crossroads itself. You are no longer merely adventurers — you are anomalies, the chosen and the cursed, carrying pieces of realms that should never meet.

And when you level, when your blood burns with new power, remember: in Skazka, it is never without cost. Every oath sworn, every spirit bound, every shadow mastered leaves a scar.

Barbarian: Path of the Shadow-Weaver



In the half-light of Skazka, rage is not just fury but unraveling. Those who walk the Path of the Shadow-Weaver draw the darkness into themselves, letting their wrath take shape as living shadow. They are half-seen, half-felt — warriors who blur at the edges, their fury whispered by the abyss.


Shadow’s Veil

Starting when you choose this path at 3rd level, dim light and darkness embrace you while you rage. While raging, you can attempt to hide even when only lightly obscured by shadows, and creatures have disadvantage on opportunity attacks against you if you start your turn in dim light or darkness.

Umbral Aura

Also at 3rd level, when you begin your rage, shadows coil around you. While you are raging and in dim light or darkness, each creature of your choice that starts its turn within 5 feet of you takes psychic damage equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum of 1).

Umbral Step

At 6th level, you can step between shadows. As a bonus action while raging, you can teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see that is in dim light or darkness. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all uses when you finish a long rest.

Creeping Dread

Beginning at 10th level, your attacks spread fear. Once per turn when you hit a creature with a melee weapon attack while raging, you can force that creature to make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier). On a failed save, it is frightened of you until the start of your next turn.

Shadowborne Fury

At 14th level, you can briefly become a creature of pure shadow. Once per rage, you can become incorporeal until the start of your next turn. While incorporeal, you have resistance to all damage except force, psychic, and radiant, and you can move through creatures and objects as if they were difficult terrain. If you end your turn inside an object, you are shunted to the nearest unoccupied space and take 1d10 force damage.

Paladin: Oath of the Crossroads



Tenets of the Crossroads

Most paladins swear to gods or crowns; these swear to the roads between. Bound by an Oath of the Crossroads, they guard the thresholds where one world touches another. They are gatekeepers and judges, drawing strength from the law of passage itself — that none may trespass unchallenged.

Paladins who swear this oath are bound to guard the thresholds between realms. Their tenets vary, but all share themes of vigilance, judgment, and balance.

  • Guard the Threshold. Protect the boundaries between worlds.
  • Judge the Intruder. No trespass goes unchallenged.
  • Hold the Balance. Do not allow one realm to overrun another.

Channel Divinity

When you take this oath at 3rd level, you gain the following two Channel Divinity options:

  • Seal the Threshold. As an action, you present your holy symbol and call upon planar seals. For 1 minute, creatures within 30 feet of you cannot teleport or travel between planes against their will unless they succeed on a Charisma saving throw against your spell save DC.
  • Banishing Smite. When you hit a creature with your Divine Smite, you can use your Channel Divinity to force it to make a Charisma saving throw. On a failure, it is banished to a harmless demiplane until the end of your next turn.

Aura of the gate

Starting at 7th level, you and friendly creatures within 10 feet of you have advantage on saving throws against effects that would teleport or forcibly move you to another plane. At 18th level, the range of this aura increases to 30 feet.

Crossroads judgment

At 15th level, you can see the truth of intruders. As an action, choose one creature you can see within 30 feet. You immediately learn its plane of origin, whether it is under a curse, and whether it is disguised or shapeshifted. Additionally, you gain advantage on your next attack roll or spell against that creature within the next minute. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

The Last Gatekeeper

At 20th level, you can assume the mantle of the eternal guardian. When you are reduced to 0 hit points, you can choose to drop to 1 hit point instead. You immediately teleport up to 30 feet to an unoccupied space you can see, and you gain temporary hit points equal to twice your paladin level. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Circle of the Whispering Wood Features

Level Feature
2nd Murmur of Leaves
6th Warding Thorns
10th Entangling Echoes
14th Verdant Requiem

Druid: Circle of the Whispering Wood



Tenets of the Crossroads

In Skazka, the forests remember. The druids of the Whispering Wood are their voices. They murmur with the roots, learn from the sap, and wield the judgment of ancient trees whose memory is older than kings. To them, growth is never gentle — it strangles as easily as it shelters.


Murmur of Leaves

When you choose this circle at 2nd level, you can communicate with plants as if under the effect of speak with plants, though the plants’ awareness is limited. In addition, when you spend at least 10 minutes in a wooded area, you gain advantage on checks to track creatures or navigate terrain within that forest.

Warding Thorns

Starting at 6th level, while you are in Wild Shape, your form sprouts spectral thorns. When a creature hits you or an ally within 10 feet of you with a melee attack, it takes piercing damage equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of 1).

Entangling Echoes

At 10th level, you can call forth vines of memory. As an action, you can force all creatures of your choice within 30 feet to make a Strength saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, a creature’s speed is reduced to 0 until the start of your next turn. You can use this feature once per short or long rest.

Verdant Requiem

At 14th level, once per long rest, you can unleash the full judgment of the forest. As an action, you conjure a burst of spectral vines in a 30-foot radius centered on yourself. Each enemy in that area must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be restrained until the start of your next turn.

Soulbind Sorcerer Features

Level Feature
1st Ancestral Echo
6th Spirit Surge
10th Shared Burden
14th Ascendant Binding

Sorcerer: Soulbind Origin



Not all sorcerers are born with wild blood or draconic veins. In Skazka, some are bound — soul to spirit, past to present. These Soulbound carry fragments of guardians, demons, or dream-echoes within them. Their power is never entirely theirs; it whispers, shields, hungers, and always demands.


Ancestral Echo

At 1st level, you are bound to a lingering spirit. Choose an affinity: Frost, Dream, or Shadow. You gain a minor benefit associated with that affinity, such as resistance to cold damage, advantage on saving throws against being charmed, or darkvision out to 60 feet.

Spirit Surge

At 6th level, when you cast a spell associated with your spirit’s theme, you can add your Charisma modifier to one damage roll or extend the spell’s duration by 1 round. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining all expended uses on a long rest.

Shared Burden

At 10th level, your spirit protects you from death. When you are reduced to 0 hit points, you can drop to 1 hit point instead. You then take necrotic damage equal to your sorcerer level at the start of your next turn. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Ascendant Binding

At 14th level, you can manifest your spirit in a spectral avatar. As an action, you summon it for 1 minute. It takes the form of a Large incorporeal ally within 10 feet, grants you advantage on saving throws against spells, and once on each of your turns, when you cast a spell, you can deal additional damage equal to your Charisma modifier to one target of that spell. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Veilstride Agent Features

Level Feature
3rd Veilstep, Ghost’s Ambush
6th Shadow slip
10th Master of Shadows
14th Pull of the rift

Rogue: Veilstride Agent



The Veilstride move where light cannot follow. In alleys, ruins, and shadowed crossings, they slip through dimness as if between worlds. Their daggers strike from impossible angles, their presence vanishes with the turning of a head. They are spies of twilight, assassins of the unseen paths.


Veilstep

At 3rd level, when you take the Hide action while in dim light or darkness, you can teleport up to 30 feet to another unoccupied space you can see that is also in dim light or darkness. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

Ghost's Ambush

Also at 3rd level, if you attack from hiding in dim light or darkness, the target has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes against your abilities before the end of your next turn.

Shadow Slip

At 6th level, when you take the Dodge action while in dim light or darkness, you also become partially incorporeal until the start of your next turn. During this time, you can move through creatures’ spaces (not walls), and opportunity attacks against you are made with disadvantage. You cannot be harmed by non-magical attacks.

Master of Shadows

At 10th level, when you are in dim light or darkness, you are invisible to creatures relying on sight to perceive you until you attack, cast a spell, or move more than half your speed.

Pull of the rift

At 14th level, your Sneak Attack tears at planar fabric. Once per turn when you deal Sneak Attack damage, the target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw (DC 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Dexterity modifier) or be stunned until the start of your next turn. Once a creature succeeds on this save, it is immune for 24 hours.

Lore of the Fading Echo Features

Level Feature
2nd Echoed Spells
6th Spectral Ward
10th Phantom Grasp
14th Temporal Slip
18th Ethereal Storm

Wizard: Lore of the Fading Echo



The wizards of the Fading Echo study not what is, but what is left behind. They weave magic from half-memories, from afterimages that linger when a spell is gone. Their art is ephemeral and haunting: illusions that echo themselves, hands that grasp from beyond, and storms that tear through the fabric of both matter and memory.


Echoed Spells

Starting at 2nd level, when you cast a 1st-level or higher wizard spell, you can choose to echo it. The spell lingers faintly in the Ethereal Plane for 1 minute. Once during that time, you or an ally within 30 feet can trigger the echo as a bonus action, replicating the spell’s effect centered on its original target. Does not work on area of effect spells. You can use this feature once per long rest. At 10th level, you can use it twice.

Spectral Ward

At 6th level, you can weave defensive echoes. As a reaction when a creature within 30 feet is targeted by a ranged attack, you can grant that creature +2 AC until the start of its next turn. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus per long rest.

Phantom Grasp

At 10th level, your mage hand can pass through solid objects and can attempt to grapple creatures within 30 feet (Strength check using your spell save DC).

Temporal Slip

At 14th level, when you fail a saving throw against a spell or magical effect, you can reroll it and must take the new result. You can use this feature once per long rest.

Ethereal Storm

At 18th level, you can unleash a storm of echoing power. As an action, choose a point within 60 feet. All creatures of your choice within a 20-foot radius must make a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, they take 8d8 force damage and are pushed 20 feet away from the point of origin; on a success, they take half damage. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Way of the Echoing Fist Features

Level Feature
3rd Echo Strike, Memory Palm
6th Recalled Motion
11th Memory Erasure
17th Thousandfold Echo

Monk: Way of the Echoing Fist



A strike is not one moment, but many. Monks of the Echoing Fist channel Skazka’s fractured memories into their bodies, leaving aftershocks of every movement. Each punch ripples across time; each palm carries erasure. In their silence, the past and present collapse into one relentless flow.


Echo Strike

Starting at 3rd level, when you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, you can spend 1 ki point to create an echo of that motion. On your next turn, before the end of your turn, you can repeat that strike against the same target as a free action: make the same attack roll again against that target. On a hit, the attack deals the normal damage for your unarmed strike (no additional modifiers beyond those that would normally apply). You can create only one echo at a time.

Memory Palm

Also at 3rd level, when you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, you can blur a sliver of its short-term memory. The target must succeed on an Intelligence saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier) or lose its reaction until the start of your next turn. You can use Memory Palm a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Recalled Motion

At 6th level, you can retrace the perfect line of a moment. As a bonus action, you can spend 1 ki point to instantly move to the space you occupied at the end of your previous turn (if it is unoccupied), without provoking opportunity attacks. If that space is occupied or invalid, this feature has no effect but the ki point is not expended.

Memory Erasure

At 11th level, when you hit the same creature with three separate unarmed strikes on your turn, you can spend 3 ki points to force it to make an Intelligence saving throw (DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier). On a failure, the creature forgets your presence until the end of your next turn: it can’t target you with attacks or spells and treats you as invisible (though area effects still affect you). The effect ends early if you deal damage to it again.

Thousandfold Echo

At 17th level, you can split a single motion into many. When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can spend 5 ki points to duplicate one unarmed strike you make with echoes that lash out at nearby foes. Choose one unarmed strike you’ve made as part of that Attack action; apply its attack roll against every creature of your choice within 10 feet of you (roll damage separately for each creature hit). You can’t apply Stunning Strike or similar effects to more than one creature with this feature.

Champion of the Lifebringer Features

Level Feature
3rd Mother’s Blessing, Vital Strikes
7th Blooming Resilience
10th Shelter of Life
15th Wellspring of Creation
18th Avatar of Uzume

Fighter: Champion of the Lifebringer



Some still call on Uzume, the murdered Vekya whose blood seeded the world. Her champions take the field as living relics—warriors whose blows carry renewal and whose presence wards against decay. To fight beside them is to feel life push back against the void.


Mother’s Blessing

Starting at 3rd level, Uzume’s echo rides your strikes. Once per turn when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can restore hit points to one ally (other than you) within 10 feet of you equal to your Constitution modifier (minimum of 1). A creature can’t benefit from this feature more than once per turn.

Vital Strikes

Also at 3rd level, your weapon attacks count as magical for the purpose of overcoming resistance and immunity to nonmagical attacks and damage.

Blooming Resilience

At 7th level, whenever you or an ally within 10 feet of you regains hit points, that creature also gains temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus. A creature can gain these temporary hit points from this feature only once per turn.

Shelter of Life

At 10th level, when a creature you can see within 15 feet of you would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can use your reaction to cause it to drop to 1 hit point instead. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

Wellspring of Creation

At 15th level, when you use your Second Wind, each ally of your choice within 20 feet of you also regains hit points equal to your fighter level.

Avatar of Uzume

At 18th level, you can manifest the Lifebringer’s grace. As a bonus action, you enter this state for 1 minute: your weapon attacks heal an ally within 10 feet of the target for an amount equal to half the damage dealt (rounded down), and you shed bright light in a 20-foot radius and dim light for 20 feet beyond. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Huntsman of the Vanishing Wilds Features

Level Feature
3rd Shadowed Quarry
7th Fading Step
11th Spirit Companion
15th Hunt the Forgotten

Ranger: Huntsman of the Vanishing Wilds



As Skazka’s forests fade and beasts slip from memory, the Huntsmen take up their calling. They stalk what leaves no tracks, listen to woods that try to forget, and walk beside spirits of leaf and shadow. Where others lose the path, the Huntsman becomes it.


Shadowed Quarry

Starting at 3rd level, when you mark a creature with hunter’s mark (or your Favored Foe), you glimpse the wild’s hidden truths. You learn whether the target is undead, construct, or extraplanar, and whether it is under a curse or memory-altering magic. While the mark persists, you have advantage on Wisdom (Survival) checks to track that creature and on Intelligence (Investigation) checks made to interpret its spoor or signs. In addition, nonmagical difficult terrain does not slow your group while you are tracking your marked quarry.

Fading Step

At 7th level, the wilds teach you to vanish between heartbeats. As a bonus action while you are in natural terrain, you become invisible until the start of your next turn or until you attack, cast a spell, or leave that terrain. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Spirit Companion

At 11th level, you call a beast-spirit of leaf and shadow to hunt by your side. As an action, you summon a spectral companion that manifests in an unoccupied space you can see within 30 feet and lasts for 1 hour, until it is reduced to 0 hit points, or until you dismiss it (no action required). The companion uses the wolf stat block, except it is a Fey, has darkvision 60 ft., and can move through the space of other creatures as if it were difficult terrain (but not through solid objects). It is resistant to nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage. The companion acts on your initiative, obeys your verbal commands, and if you issue none, it takes the Dodge action. Once you summon the companion, you can’t do so again until you finish a long rest, or you can expend a 2nd-level spell slot to summon it again.

Hunt the Forgotten

At 15th level, your strikes punish things that memory can’t hold. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you deal an extra 2d8 psychic damage if the target is immune to being charmed or frightened, or if it is an undead or construct. A creature can’t take this extra damage from you more than once per turn.

College of Fading Song Features

Level Feature
3rd Song of Silence, Erasure Inspiration
6th Dissonant Echo
14th Final Verse

Bard: College of Fading Song



Not every song is meant to endure. Bards of the Fading Song weave laments that unravel memory and hush the proud. Their voices summon choirs of the forgotten, gnawing at the edges of thought. To hear them is to feel beauty fade— and to know that nothing lasts.


Song of Silence

Starting at 3rd level, you can spend a use of your Bardic Inspiration as a bonus action to loose a note that eats sound. A 10-foot-radius sphere of magical hush centers on you until the start of your next turn. Within the area, verbal components can’t be uttered and creatures are deafened. Creatures and objects can still be targeted normally outside of the normal silence spell’s total sound negation—this is a brief, choking hush, not an environmental seal. If you move, the sphere moves with you. You can’t use this feature if you are unable to speak or produce sound.

Erasure Inspiration

Also at 3rd level, when a creature uses your Bardic Inspiration die on an ability check, attack roll, or saving throw, it can choose to end one of the following conditions affecting it: charmed, frightened, grappled, restrained, deafened. The condition ends immediately after the die is rolled. A creature can benefit from this erasure only once per short or long rest.

Dissonant Echo

At 6th level, your fading refrain lingers as a psychic burr. When a creature you can see within 60 feet expends a Bardic Inspiration die you granted, choose one enemy you can see within 60 feet that can hear you. That enemy must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw against your spell save DC or take psychic damage equal to one roll of your Bardic Inspiration die and has disadvantage on the next saving throw it makes before the end of your next turn. You can trigger this feature a number of times equal to your Charisma modifier (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Final Verse

At 14th level, you learn the song that outlives the singer. Once per long rest, when you drop to 0 hit points or when you take an action on your turn, you can begin the Final Verse, creating a 30-foot aura of resonant memory for 1 minute. While in the aura, allies of your choice gain advantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened, and once during the duration each affected ally can roll your Bardic Inspiration die and add the number rolled to one attack roll or saving throw (this doesn’t expend your Bardic Inspiration). The song persists even if you fall unconscious, but it ends early if you die.

Domain of the Ancient Mother Features

Level Feature
1st Bonus Proficiencies, Gift of Renewal
2nd Channel Divinity: Restore Memory
6th Aura of Growth
8th Divine Strike
17th Mother’s Rebirth

Cleric: Domain of the Ancient Mother



Clerics of the Ancient Mother tend the last green spark of Uzume, the murdered Lifebringer. Their prayers coax sap to rise and memory to return; their wrath chokes rot and gnaws at entropy. To walk with them is to feel the world insist on living.


Bonus Proficiencies

When you choose this domain at 1st level, you gain proficiency in Nature and with the herbalism kit. You also learn the druidcraft cantrip if you don’t already know it.

Gift of Renewal

Also at 1st level, Uzume’s remnant answers your healing magic. When you cast a spell of 1st level or higher that restores hit points to a creature, you can choose one of the following additional effects on that target: end one disease; end the poisoned, frightened, or charmed condition; or end one magical effect that specifically alters or erases the creature’s memories (DM’s discretion). You can use this feature a number of times equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of once), and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Channel Divinity: Restore Memory

Starting at 2nd level, you can use your Channel Divinity to knit torn recollection. As an action, you touch a willing creature and restore a single memory that was altered, erased, or blocked by magic within the last 24 hours (as if ending the effect of a modify memory spell for that incident). If the memory loss predates the last 24 hours, the creature instead recalls one clear fragment or image related to the lost memory (DM’s discretion). This does not reveal information the creature never possessed.

Aura of Growth

At 6th level, life gathers around you. You and friendly creatures within 10 feet of you who start their turns at half their hit point maximum or fewer regain hit points equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum of 1). A creature can benefit from this aura only once per turn. At 17th level, the radius of this aura increases to 30 feet.

Divine Strike

At 8th level, you gain the ability to infuse your weapon strikes with vital radiance. Once on each of your turns when you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can cause the attack to deal an extra 1d8 radiant damage. When you reach 14th level, the extra damage increases to 2d8.

Mother’s Rebirth

At 17th level, you can turn the moment of loss into life. When a creature you can see within 30 feet would be reduced to 0 hit points, you can use your reaction to have it drop to 1 hit point instead. When you do, you can also end one of the following conditions on that creature: blinded, deafened, paralyzed, or poisoned. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Pact of the Void Drakesoul Features

Level Feature
1st Void Breath, Entropic Resistance
6th Silence of Fafnirog
10th Memory Devourer
14th Wings of the Void

Warlock: Pact of the Void Drakesoul



Some supplicate to gods or fiends; others bind themselves to absence itself. Those who swear to the shadow of Fafnirog — the Void Dragon who feasts on light and memory — learn to exhale silence, drink secrets, and wear wings of nothingness. Their magic does not blaze; it erases.


Void Breath

Starting at 1st level, you can exhale a gout of un-light. As an action, you unleash a 15-foot cone of void. Each creature in the area must make a Constitution saving throw against your spell save DC, taking 1d6 force damage on a failed save, or half as much on a success. Nonmagical light sources in the cone are snuffed out. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

The damage increases to 2d6 at 3rd level, 3d6 at 5th level, and 4d6 at 7th level.

Entropic Resistance

Also at 1st level, you harden your flesh against the void. Choose necrotic or psychic damage. You gain resistance to the chosen type. You can change this choice whenever you finish a long rest.

Silence of Fafnirog

At 6th level, the void answers your call. You can cast silence centered on a point you can see within 60 feet without expending a spell slot or material components. When cast this way, the spell’s duration is concentration, up to 1 minute, and the sphere is visibly rimmed by a thin ring of black-violet embers. You can use this feature once per short or long rest.

Memory Devourer

At 10th level, when a creature you can see within 30 feet drops to 0 hit points, you can use your reaction to siphon its fading thoughts. You gain temporary hit points equal to your Charisma modifier + your warlock level, and the next time you make an ability check or saving throw before the end of your next turn, you do so with advantage. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Wings of the Void

At 14th level, you unfurl wings of living shadow. As a bonus action, you gain a flying speed of 60 feet and immunity to the frightened condition for 1 minute. While this feature is active, dim light within 10 feet of you is considered darkness. Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.

Order of the Primal Scourge Features

Level Feature
3rd Rite of the Primals, Primal Litany
7th Lifeblood Transference
11th Scourging Aura
15th Avatar of Blood and Seed

Blood Hunter: Order of the Primal Scourge



The Primal Scourge bleed not only to harm, but to seed. Their rites mingle hemocraft with the last green spark of Uzume, turning pain into plenty and wielding life as a weapon against rot and void. Where they strike, wounds bloom; where they stand, allies rise again.


Rite of the Primals

When you choose this order at 3rd level, you learn the Crimson Rite of the Primals. When you activate your Crimson Rite, you can choose this rite to infuse your weapon strikes with radiant vigor. While this rite is active, your rite damage type is radiant instead of the chosen type, and once on each of your turns when you deal your Crimson Rite damage to a creature, you can cause one ally (other than you) within 10 feet of the target to regain hit points equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of 1). A creature can benefit from this healing only once per turn. This feature otherwise follows all normal rules for Crimson Rite.

Primal Litany

Also at 3rd level, you learn druidic fragments that steady the hand that bleeds. You gain proficiency in the Nature skill or Herbalism Kit (your choice). In addition, when you finish a short rest, you can choose yourself or one creature you touch; until the start of your next turn, the chosen creature has advantage on the next death saving throw it makes.

Lifeblood Transference

At 7th level, you can turn your suffering into a bulwark. Whenever you take hemocraft damage from activating a Crimson Rite or from amplifying a Blood Curse, you can choose to take an additional 1d6 hemocraft damage. If you do, choose one creature you can see within 30 feet; it gains temporary hit points equal to the total hemocraft damage you took from that activation or amplification. You can use this feature a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, and you regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Scourging Aura

Beginning at 11th level, while your Crimson Rite is active, you exude a faint, pollen-like radiance that unsettles the faithless and the hollow. Creatures of your choice that start their turn within 10 feet of you have disadvantage on saving throws against being charmed or frightened until the start of their next turn. In addition, creatures have disadvantage on saving throws against your Blood Curses while they are within 10 feet of you. A creature that succeeds on a saving throw against one of your Blood Curses is immune to this secondary disadvantage from your aura until the end of its next turn.

Avatar of Blood and Seed

At 15th level, you can erupt with the Lifebringer’s echo. As a bonus action while your Crimson Rite is active, you can assume the avatar for 1 minute. For the duration:

  • Your weapon attacks deal an extra 1d8 radiant damage.
  • Allies of your choice that start their turn within 20 feet of you gain 1d6 temporary hit points.
  • When you reduce a hostile creature to 0 hit points, you can immediately choose one ally within 20 feet to regain hit points equal to your Intelligence modifier (minimum of 1). A creature can benefit from this healing only once per round.

Once you use this feature, you can’t use it again until you finish a long rest.
