Overview

Agramaul stands as perhaps the most solemn and impartial of all divine beings, serving as the eternal Gatekeeper to the afterlife and the ultimate Judge of Souls who determines the fate of every mortal spirit that passes from the realm of the living. Unlike other deities who may be swayed by worship, bribes, or personal relationships, Agramaul operates with absolute moral authority and unwavering dedication to cosmic justice, weighing each soul against the sum total of their actions, intentions, and the impact they had upon the world during their mortal existence. Neither cruel nor merciful in the traditional sense, Agramaul embodies perfect justice - a concept that transcends mortal understanding of fairness and operates according to universal principles that account for circumstance, intention, capability, and consequence in ways that ensure each soul receives exactly what their life's choices have earned them. Standing at the threshold between life and death, Agramaul serves not only as judge but also as guardian, ensuring that no soul passes to its final destination without proper evaluation, and that the cosmic balance between good and evil, justice and chaos, remains eternally maintained. Those who have experienced near-death encounters describe meeting a figure of terrible beauty and infinite wisdom, whose very presence strips away all pretense and deception, leaving souls bare before the ultimate accounting of their existence.

Influence

  • Death: Ease the dying, speak with waiting souls, keep the dead at rest, and bar undeath.
  • Justice: Perceive lies, compel truth, expose corruption, and deliver rightful consequence while shielding the innocent.
  • Knowledge: Consult the soul-ledger—read the dead’s memories, weigh consequences across time, and counsel with clear moral insight.

Symbols

  • Scales of Truth: Perfectly balanced scales—heart vs. white feather—weighing deed and intent in Agramaul’s golden light.
  • Eternal Gate: Black-stone doorway open to a star-swirled cosmos; rune-names of all who passed—the threshold of death and its infinite after.
  • All-Seeing Eye: A blazing central eye ringed by many eyes—sight through all deceit to a soul’s true moral weight.

Tenets

  • Face the Ledger: Speak truth of deed and intent; accept consequence without plea.
  • Honor the Passage: Guard the boundary of life and death; abhor undeath and exploitative necromancy.
  • Judge Impartially: No bribes, no grudges—measure all by the same scale.

Rites

  • Final Confession: For the dying—incense clears self-deceit; the soul speaks every remembered choice as a priest records. Not absolution, but clear-eyed reckoning to ease judgment.
  • Trial of the Living: When law fails—stand before Agramaul’s altar: truth-binding oaths, guiltless flames, and visions of those harmed/helped. Verdict shows as visible stigma or protective blessing.
  • Weighing of Consequences: Before a grave decision—seven-day fast, then place symbols of each option on a replica of the Scales. Through prayer and vision, gain moral foresight, not easy answers.

Lore

The Great Reckoning: When cosmic balance falters, Agramaul closes the Gates and judges every soul—living and dead—against the complete moral ledger. Fates may be revised. Aftermath varies by creed: either the afterlife is remade or the life–death cycle ends as perfect moral clarity makes judgment unnecessary.
