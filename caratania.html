Politics

Caratania is a monarchy ruled by King Alaric IV, whose dynasty claims direct descent from Hrothgar Bloodlance himself. While the crown commands loyalty, it is the Church of Aeter that truly shapes Caratanian life. The Church, through its grand clergy and militant Shield of Dawn, wields enormous influence over law, custom, and governance. Priests serve as judges, inquisitors, and advisors to the nobility, ensuring that both throne and people remain steadfast in their devotion. This balance between monarch and church has forged a state where piety is inseparable from politics, and faith is not a private matter but the very lifeblood of public order.

Population

Caratania counts a population of roughly 400,000 souls. The heart of the kingdom is the fortified city of Caratania itself, home to the royal court, and the high clergy. Beyond the city lie dozens of towns and villages scattered across the foothills of the Blood Mountains.

Caratania is a brooding kingdom of mist-laden valleys and shadowed mountains, perched at the foot of the forbidding Blood Mountains. Its people are the descendants of warriors and martyrs, heirs to the unyielding defiance of Hrothgar Bloodlance and his companions who cast down the vampire lords of old. This land is one of superstition and faith, where every hearth bears charms against the restless dead and every household whispers prayers to Aeter, the Eternal Flame, before nightfall. Yet Caratania is not only a kingdom of vigilant hearts; it is also a land of rich vineyards and crimson wine, whose deep taste is said to carry both the sweetness of life and the memory of blood. It is a realm where legend and reality are woven so tightly that the people live in constant awe of their own history.

The Cathedral of the Eternal Flame

A fortress-like basilica dedicated to Aeter. Its black stone walls rise like cliffs above the city, its spires crowned with perpetual torches said never to extinguish. Both citadel and sanctuary, it houses relics of the vampire wars and serves as the seat of the High Flamekeeper.

The Royal Palace of House Bloodlance

A solemn stronghold at the heart of Caratania City, where King Alaric IV and his court rule. Its walls are adorned with tapestries recounting the slaying of vampire lords.

Visiting Caratania

Travelers who enter Caratania will find themselves in a land both beautiful and foreboding. The vineyards produce some of the most celebrated wines in all of Kelos, rich and dark, favored by nobles and merchants alike. Visitors are often struck by the strong religious character of the people—processions wind through the streets, bells toll at every hour, and even the inns bear symbols of Aeter to ward off evil. Foreigners are welcomed warmly enough, but always with an edge of watchfulness, for strangers might carry shadows with them. The nights are long in Caratania, and no traveler forgets the weight of unseen eyes when the moonlight falls upon the mountains.

The Shield of Dawn Barracks

Headquarters of the militant arm of the Church, where knights train relentlessly to stand as humanity’s bulwark against the dark.

The Moonlit Pass

A treacherous mountain road said to be haunted by the spirits of the vampire legions who once marched there. Travelers speak of ghostly lights flickering in the mist.

The Pilgrim’s Market

A bustling square within Caratania City, filled with relic-sellers, wine merchants, and holy charms blessed by priests of Aeter.

The Ossuary of Saint Hrothgar

A sacred crypt holding the remains of Hrothgar Bloodlance and his companions, visited by pilgrims who seek blessings from their bones.
