Koshchei

Patron of the wronged

Profile

Classification Lesser Power
Aliases The Withered Bridegroom
Domains Vengance, Secrets, Decay
Favoured The wronged, Executioners, Prisoners
Alignment Lawful Neutral
Holy Symbol A blackened heart
Known forms A withered bridegroom in burial blacks who arrives where grievances curdle

Overview

Koshchei, sometimes called The Withered Bridegroom or The Immortal Spite, is a demi-god who dwells in the borderlands between life and death, feeding on bitterness, broken promises, and betrayed love. Unlike greater gods, Koshchei does not rule over temples or nations; instead, he whispers to the wronged and dispossessed, offering them the cold comfort of retribution.

To his worshippers—known as Brides and Grooms—Koshchei represents both justice and cruelty: a force that balances the scales when mortal laws fail. He is neither wholly evil nor merciful, but an arbiter of pain, weaving vengeance into the destiny of those who call upon him.

Koshchei is said to appear as a gaunt, pale man draped in black funeral garb, his eyes hollow yet burning faintly with silver fire. In his hands, he always holds something bound—threads, chains, or a heart of glass. He is both jailer and liberator, an immortal who cannot die because his soul is hidden beyond reach.

Influence

  • Vengeance: The settling of debts and wrongs unpunished by mortal courts.
  • Secrets of the soul: He is keeper of concealed truths, oaths, and the soul-vessels that bind immortality.
  • Decay and withering: The slow wasting away of vitality, beauty, and hope—yet also the breaking down of false facades.

Symbols

  • Black Coin: Badge of his order—one face a pierced heart, the other strangling hands—both warning and inevitability of his vengeance.
  • Bound Thread: Black knotted cord used in rites to bind a wrongdoer’s fate and tether their soul to Koshchei’s will.
  • Hollow Heart: Cracked vessel (bone, glass, or obsidian) signifying betrayal and emptiness—a shadow of the vessel hiding Koshchei’s own soul.

Tenets

  • Debts Must Ripen: Broken oaths and betrayed love are paid in full; mercy without restitution is a lie.
  • Mark the Guilty: Bind their fate—coin delivered, cord knotted, heart hollowed—until the balance returns.
  • Let Decay Unmask: What is false must wither; strip away facades until the soul’s truth stands bare.

Rites

  • Gift of the Black Coin: A Bride/Groom blood-marks a black coin and delivers it; from that moment, the condemned belongs to Koshchei.
  • Knotting of Spite: While the wronged recounts their grief, a black cord is knotted and hidden near the target; while the knots hold, the victim withers.
  • Moon’s Witness: If unpaid by the eighth dawn, smear blood on the hollow heart under a waning moon—Koshchei claims their life before the new moon.

Avatars

A withered bridegroom

Lore

Old tales whisper that Koshchei cannot be slain, for his soul is hidden in an egg inside a bird, inside a beast, inside a box—buried deep where no hand may reach.
