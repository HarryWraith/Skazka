Physical Description

While the provided histories focus more on culture than appearance, Orcs carry the physical legacy of their creation as soldiers. Their frames are built for endurance and strength, making them imposing figures in both tribal and urban settings. Their physicality, honed for survival in hostile worlds and endless battlefields, often fuels the reputation that they are brutish or violent. Yet this same form is equally suited to guardianship, labor, and resilience, reflecting a people shaped for struggle but capable of adapting to new roles when necessity demands.

The Orcs are a people whose very existence is defined by their origins as weapons of war. Unlike the other races of Skazka, who evolved or emerged naturally within its borders, the Orcs were deliberately created by the dark god Chernobog to serve as soldiers in battles beyond this world. Once the great wars ended and their god’s cause was concluded, they found themselves stranded on a dying world beneath a fading sun. In a rare moment of divine mercy, Chernobog transported the remnants of his warriors to Skazka, offering them refuge in recognition of their devotion and service. Though they were born for battle, their arrival in Skazka marked the beginning of an uncertain future—a new chapter where their purpose had to be redefined.

Heritage

The Orcish heritage is steeped in oral tradition, sectiontained by their storytellers, the Saga-Keepers. Through spoken history, they preserve the memory of their dying homeworld and the cosmic battles that defined their earliest existence. These tales are not only acts of remembrance but also of identity: they acknowledge the sacrifices of those who fell in wars not of their choosing and remind every generation that survival is itself a victory. Their stories speak of impossible battlefields—cities adrift on molten seas, wars waged in the light of dying stars—and of Chernobog himself, not as a distant god, but as a commanding presence who once walked among them. This heritage, rooted in both mythic grandeur and hard-won survival, resections the foundation of their cultural identity.

Motivation

For Orcs, survival and endurance are the greatest victories. Their culture values loyalty over convenience, strength tempered by adaptability, and perseverance in the face of overwhelming odds. Having witnessed the collapse of a world and the death of its sun, Orcs carry with them a profound understanding of impermanence. This perspective fuels their drive to find meaning in new forms of existence, whether as tribal guardians defending their chosen lands or as urban settlers striving for acceptance in foreign societies. Their desires are not limited to conquest or bloodshed; rather, they yearn for purpose, whether that means safeguarding their people, honoring their heritage, or proving their worth in the eyes of others.

Integration into Society

Orcs exist in Skazka as both tribal warriors and urban settlers, each path reflecting different approaches to survival. Tribal Orcs live in scattered groups across remote regions, where their territorial instincts can be channeled into protecting rather than conquering land. Their societies sectiontain military hierarchies, but in practice, they often act as guardians of wilderness frontiers, deterring monsters and encroachment alike. Outsiders may misinterpret their directness and comfort with violence as savagery, but within their own culture, these traits are tempered by discipline and an understanding of defense rather than needless aggression.

At the same time, a minority of Orcs choose to leave their tribes and integrate into cities, despite facing deep-seated prejudice. These urban Orcs find employment in roles that allow them to contribute while drawing on their natural strengths—guards, builders, guides, and protectors of trade routes. For them, integration is not abandonment of heritage but a conscious adaptation to ensure their people’s survival in a changing world. While they endure mistrust and discrimination, they view these struggles as another battle, one to be met with persistence instead of violence. In doing so, they slowly reshape perceptions, demonstrating that Orcs can be more than soldiers—that they can build, protect, and live alongside others without losing their essence.
