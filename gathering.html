Design Intent

Gathering is designed to make herbalism, skinning, foraging and hunting feel like an adventure activity, not a shopping trip. It adds flavor, rewards exploration, and scales with rarity—while staying light on mechanics so it never drags down play.

Guiding Principles

  • Incidental, Not Downtime – Gathering happens naturally during travel or exploration; it should never slow the game or consume separate adventuring time.
  • Environment – The type of terrain determines what can be found.
  • Skill + Tools Matter – Characters roll Nature/Survival checks and add proficiency with the right tools, rewarding expertise in exploration and crafting.


House rules: Gathering

While traveling in the wilderness, a character with an herbalism kit (or similar expertise) may attempt a gathering check (usually Wisdom (Survival) or Intelligence (Nature)) to find useful ingredients. This check represents casually picking herbs, roots, mushrooms, salts or crystals along the way. In game terms, allow one such check per day of travel (or per suitable exploration period) without spending extra hours – it’s an incidental action, not a full “Downtime” day.

When & How


While traveling in the wilderness, a character with the right tools and skills may attempt a gathering check to find useful ingredients. This check represents casually picking herbs, roots, mushrooms or hunting small game for skins & meat. In game terms, allow one such check per day of travel (or per suitable exploration period) without spending extra hours – it’s an incidental action, not a full “Downtime” day.

The final yield is determined by the DM and should reflect the actual availability of the resource. Even an excellent skill check cannot produce a large harvest if the resource is naturally scarce; likewise, abundant environments may reward greater yields on a success.

Measuring success


Gathering requires a skill check against a DC, which can be modified by environmental factors such as terrain, season, or outside influences (e.g. a nearby wolf pack). On a success, the character collects usable reagents. The exact Yield is determined by how successful the skill check was. Consult the Yield table at the bottom of this page.. here.

Skill checks & DC


The base DC for all gathering checks is 10. This DC is then adjusted by situational factors such as terrain, season, or other complications that make gathering more difficult. Skinning requires proficiency with Leatherworker’s Tools. Each attempt calls for a skill check, though the hunter and the skinner need not be the same person. Of course, if the hunt fails, there is nothing to skin. Consult the table below for suggested Yield. The chosen Yield for skinning must match the result of the hunt. For example: if Cedric the hunter secures a successful Yield of 2 and chooses to roll on the Uncommon table, Bob—the party’s skinner—may then attempt to skin the creature.

Keep in mind, however, that a successful hunt does not guarantee a successful skinning. Further, the DC for skinning is based solely on the rarity of the creature that was successfully hunted. Each individual beast you wish to skin requires its own skill check.

Yield


Consult the table below. Once a Yield is determined, proceed to the generators here. The Yield column provides a numerical value that represents the total number of rolls the player can make.

Note that when hunting or foraging, the Yield also represents how many people can be sustained for one day. For example, a Yield of 3 from hunting combined with 3 from foraging provides a total Yield of 6—enough to feed 6 people for one day, or 3 people for two days.

Note: if your travel speed exceeds 'normal' pace then you cannot hunt or forage. If the party elects to move at a slower speed then foraging and hunting checks can be made with advantage.
