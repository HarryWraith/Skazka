Origins

Established deep within mountain folds, Kitezh was crafted from grey granite and revered for its masterful works—vaulted halls, carved effigies, and runic architecture.

Decline

Beneath the weight of their contract to be the smiths of the gods the dwarves endured—until they rose up. Their wrath consumed large parts of the city; but some of it remains intact.

The city's end was abrupt and brutal. The dwarves were kept in check by their own kind; dwarves known as duergar (betrayers) and a garrison of Kua Toa. Once the legions of dwarves had put their mind to revolt it was all but over.

Once the heart of a powerful necromantic sect, the Ruins of Orishan lie amid the shifting dunes of formerly fertile Kaijistan, on the banks of a long-dry river bed. As a living city, Orishan stood proud in the Kingdom of Kaijistan; now, its skeleton stands half-buried in wind-blown sand, a grim warning to those who dare to enter.

Though many adventurers have attempted to carve their names into its crumbling stone, few return — and those who do tell hushed tales of whispers in the corridors and undead shadows moving beneath the moon.

Signs of former power and glory lie everywhere.

Dangers & Rumours

  1. Frequent rockslides may entomb unwary explorers forever. Cursed runic wards might trigger—snuffing out torches, triggering tremors, or animating stone fragments. Lingering Slave Chains: Some explorers claim to have seen spectral chains dragging across the rubble, echoing the bondage once suffered by the dwarves. Touching them leaves bruises that never fade.
  2. Echoes of the Rebellion: Whispers of battle still ring through the halls — the clash of hammers, the cries of revolt. Those who linger too long may suddenly find themselves surrounded by ghostly scenes of the uprising. Few return after witnessing them.
  3. Unstable Tunnels: The dwarves collapsed many passages during the rebellion. The mountain remains treacherous — walls crumble, ceilings groan, and entire corridors can vanish under sudden rockslides.
  4. Stone That Watches: Statues of dwarves line the ruins. Some are broken, others eerily intact. Travelers swear their eyes follow intruders, and in rare cases the statues move to block paths or crush trespassers.
  5. The Curse of the Ashes: Local folklore insists that breathing the dust of Kitezh dooms the lungs — many who leave the ruins are struck by a slow suffocation, as though the mountain still chokes them.
  6. Treasure of Betrayal: A rumour persists of a hidden vault containing weapons forged before the uprising. Every expedition that has sought them has ended in disaster — leading many to believe the weapons themselves thirst for betrayal and blood.

The Ruins Today

Even reaching Kitezh is an achievement. The city lies five days’ journey below the surface, buried deep within the bones of the mountain. Adventurers must brave the endless dark, winding through lightless caverns where every step echoes like a warning. The most direct path cuts through the dominion of the drow, whose watchful eyes never miss intruders, and whose ambushes are swift and merciless. Beyond their shadowed halls lie tunnels teeming with monstrous denizens: chittering swarms, blind stone-hunters, and great cavern beasts that stalk without sound. Supplies dwindle, torches burn low, and madness creeps at the edges of the mind long before the first glimpse of Kitezh’s shattered gates.

Those who reach her gates face a tomb of rock and sorrow, but truth known, so few have returned from those depths that little is known other than rumour and supposition.

  • Collapsed Grand Halls filled with overturned pillars and broken mosaics of dwarven runes, half-buried in dust and cinders.
  • Embers in the Stone: some stones appear scorched, carrying faint glowing sigils—possibly wards or curses still alive.
  • Hidden Vaults glimpsed through crevices in the rubble; dwarven tools and relics might remain intact, but unstable rock threatens to seal them forever.
  • Stone Sentinels: fractured statues of dwarven heroes line collapsed corridors—some say they shift if your back is turned.
