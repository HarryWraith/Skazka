Vineta

The Sunken Tsardom—blessed by Njord, judged by the sea.

Overview

There was an island once—bright sails in a ring of calm, markets rich with amber, salt, and song. Njord, the sea’s fair keeper, blessed Vineta for its seamanship and honest dealing. Winds favored its masts, fish ran to its nets, and no storm set its teeth upon those shores without leave.

But prosperity fattened into pride. The islanders tithed less to Njord and more to darker altars raised in cellars and salt-caves. Night-feasts rang the Nine Harbor Bells to other names. When oaths turned false and weights in the market grew heavy with lies, Njord judged. He did not slay the people: mercy matched justice. At a single dawn-toll the sea rose, and Vineta sank—its towers drinking moonlight under green water. The folk were changed so they might live with what they had done: men and women grew into Vodyanoy and Rusalki, keepers and singers of the drowned city, bound to tides and truth.

Now Vineta is a place of stories. Sometimes a fisherman hears bells toll from a windless horizon; sometimes a blue lamp glides beneath a skiff and a pale hand places a coin of saltglass upon a sleeper’s tongue. On midnights of great need—or great deceit—the city draws near to the surface like a memory rising from deep water. There is no road to it. Those who claim to have walked its colonnades speak of kelp cathedrals and whale-bone bridges, of markets held in silence where promises are weighed like pearls. They return with wet hair and eyes full of tide, remembering only that they were forgiven—or that they were not.

Districts

  • Nine Bells Quarter — the old market ring; bell-towers now chime through the sea.
  • Salt-King’s Harbor — drowned docks where lamplight wanders like will-o’-the-waves.
  • Whalebone Causey — a rib-arched avenue linking the palace mound to the basilica.

Factions & Orders

  • Drowned Choir — Rusalki who judge in currents and song.
  • Wreckwardens — Vodyanoy guardians of gates, nets, and true weights.
  • Veilkeepers — those who tend the lamps and keep Vineta veiled from the world.

Economy & Trade

  • Vineta does not trade. At times, cursed coins and saltglass tokens wash ashore after storms.
  • Folk tales claim a true confession cast into the sea returns as a small blessing on the next tide.

Rumours & hooks

  • One day a year the city will rise above the sea.
  • Weights stolen from Vineta’s markets make every bargain sour until returned.
  • A bell-clapper taken from the Nine rings will toll once for each lie told nearby.

Places of Note

The Nine Bells

Nine bronze towers that chime beneath the waves. Each bell tolls a different kind of truth—debt, oath, mercy, and more. Liars hear only silence.

Njord’s Weir

A crescent of standing stones now reef-crowned. Currents curl here like braided ropes; petitions cast between the pillars are carried into fair winds—or sunk forever.

The Drowned Basilica

Once the pride of the island. Its murals of fair trade are overgrown with kelp; the Drowned Choir sing judgments from its shadowed aisles.

The Salt Throne

A crystalline seat accreted from centuries of sea-salt. No one sits there long; the sea weighs every word spoken upon it.

The Mirror Steps

Stairs descending into a plaza whose water shows what was promised rather than what is. Many turn back; some step forward and are forgiven.

The Wrecker’s Gate

A shattered arch where false beacons were once lit. The Wreckwardens keep it dark, and the currents there run sharp as knives.
