Origins

Orishan was founded during Kaijistan’s more temperate era, along a now-vanished river that once sustained orchards and gardens. The necromancers who made it their home chose its location deliberately: near water, near old trade routes, and yet remote enough to avoid unwanted eyes.

Decline

As the climate shifted and the river dried, the life that sustained Orishan withered. The necromancers’ death-binding magic, once a source of power, may have ultimately contributed to its undoing: energies unchecked, magical backlash, perhaps even revolt by spirits or undead. The city was abandoned, left to the sands, but not to silence.

Once the heart of a powerful necromantic sect, the Ruins of Orishan lie amid the shifting dunes of formerly fertile Kaijistan, on the banks of a long-dry river bed. As a living city, Orishan stood proud in the Kingdom of Kaijistan; now, its skeleton stands half-buried in wind-blown sand, a grim warning to those who dare to enter.

Though many adventurers have attempted to carve their names into its crumbling stone, few return — and those who do tell hushed tales of whispers in the corridors and undead shadows moving beneath the moon.

“Venture not into its halls, for the sands remember.”
– local proverb

Dangers & Rumours

The Ruins of Orishan are notoriously hazardous, even to well-prepared parties:

  1. Frequent sandstorms blast across the dry river bed, sometimes uncovering buried corridors — and sometimes burying them again in hours.
  2. Magical traps left by the necromancers (pressure plates, glyph wards) continue to trigger unpredictably, sometimes summoning skeletal guardians or releasing sudden gusts of choking sand.
  3. The undead are said to wander: skeletal servants, ghoulish forms half-buried, animated bone-dust swirls. Some adventurers claim to have seen larger constructs loom in moonlit corridors.
  4. The local tribes consider Orishan cursed. They warn travellers: any treasure removed from the ruins carries a lingering curse, bringing misfortune, nightmares, or worse. Those who survive return afflicted by strange dreams or disease-like symptoms.
  5. More than one search party has vanished entirely; local lore suggests that some corridors rearrange themselves, or lead into hidden subterranean chambers from which no one emerges.

The Ruins Today

Orishan is a place of eerie beauty and unquiet dread. The dry river bed that skirts the ruins is now a cracked ribbon of worn stone, but during rare seasons, water may trickle through — though no living thing seems drawn to it.

The Sand-choked Courtyard

Once a grand plaza with statues depicting necromancers in hooded robes and stylised skeletal figures, now half buried in dunes. Occasionally, wind exposes fragments of broken marble, some carved with half-erased glyphs.

The Ossuary Hall

A long corridor lined with niches that once held skulls, bones, and bone-crafted reliquaries. Some niches remain, others are empty. Adventurers report a faint rattling of small bones shifting in the gloom.

The Bone Glyph Chambers

Off the main hall lie several small chambers whose walls are carved with mystic runes inlaid with powdered bone dust. Some glyphs glow faintly in moonlight, and rumour has it they can animate skeletons from the sand.

The Whispering Catacombs

Beneath the visible ruins lie catacombs partly filled by sand. Ancient wooden coffins, warped and rotted, rest among broken sarcophagi. Some say you can hear low, ghostly murmurs when the wind blows just right.

The Necromancer’s Spire

Once a lighthouse-like tower rising above the city, its upper portion collapsed centuries ago. The lower spiral stair remains, leading into darkness. A few broken altars remain, stained and etched with dried blood and strange glyphic symbols.

“No man who walks its halls escapes wholly untouched. The bones remember your footsteps.”
— Saying among the desert tribes

The native tribes regard Orishan with deep suspicion. Many refuse to accept goods recovered from its ruins, claiming the curse travels with them. Some elders speak of dreams haunted by undead forms; others whisper that thieves who steal relics bring back misfortune to their whole clan.

Some tribal legends suggest that the necromancers once invoked a curse on the city itself, binding restless spirits to the sands; thus, even buried sections of the ruins might respond to intruders.
