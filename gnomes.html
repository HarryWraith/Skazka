Overview

The Gnomes of Skazka are a people wrapped in mystery, their origins obscured in the mists of history. Unlike Elves, whose ancestry stretches clearly back to ancient lineages, or Dwarves, who can trace their roots to divine design, Gnomes seem to have simply appeared — a quiet people without fanfare, mythic event, or cosmic purpose. This lack of a definitive origin has become part of their identity, shaping them into a race defined less by destiny and more by ingenuity, adaptability, and a relentless drive to improve the world around them. Where others rely on tradition, brute strength, or divine blessing, Gnomes excel through innovation, subtle progress, and the quiet revolution of everyday invention.

At the heart of every Gnome lies an insatiable curiosity and an unshakable desire to solve problems. To them, the world is not something to be endured, but a puzzle waiting for solutions. Their smiths design weapons and tools that adapt to conditions, their alchemists brew concoctions that make life safer and easier, and their engineers devise mechanisms that save lives in mines or make simple tasks more efficient. Gnomes are not driven by conquest, wealth, or glory—though these may come as side effects—but by the joy of discovery and the satisfaction of creating something that improves the lives of others. They embody the belief that progress need not come from sacrifice, but from dedication, creativity, and the clever application of knowledge.

Gnomes fit seamlessly into the wider fabric of Skazkan society, though always in their own distinctive way. Their underground communities are masterworks of space management and clever engineering, thriving around natural resources and built with astonishing efficiency. Surface settlements, meanwhile, tend to appear in neglected corners of larger towns—areas too cramped or inconvenient for others, which Gnomes transform into vibrant districts filled with workshops, markets, and ingenious innovations. Their culture, unlike many others, values merit above bloodline, with guilds and workshops granting status based on skill and contribution rather than birthright. This meritocracy produces brilliant merchants, teachers, and craftsmen who spread their creations far and wide. Though peaceful by inclination, Gnomes are not defenseless; when threatened, their ingenuity turns to strategy, traps, and tactical warfare. In both war and peace, they prove that progress and survival alike rest on the same foundation—intelligence, creativity, and adaptability.

Physiology & Traits

Gnomes are small, sharp-eyed, and quick-fingered—warm earth-toned skin, lively features, wiry hair (often with whiskers), and pocketed layers crammed with tools and trinkets. Dimorphism reads clearly in silhouette: males a touch taller with squarer shoulders, heavier brows, deeper voices, and the fuller beards; females shorter on average with softer features, rounder hips, finer body hair, and lighter voices—yet both remain compact, deft, and restless with motion. Their senses live in the fine print: dim and darkness open into soft greys, tiny sounds—gear teeth kissing, a latch settling—pop to attention, and a keen nose sorts oils, filings, and singe so a workshop “speaks” like a book.

Their gifts are nimble rather than grand. Gnomish wits slip past charms and glamours as through a service door, and most carry a bloodline knack: forest gnomes weave whispers and light into harmless illusions and gossip with sparrows and squirrels; rock gnomes seem to “remember” how things work—compounds, tolerances, old mechanism quirks—and can coax clever clockworks from springs and wire. Small in frame but tireless in curiosity, they turn ingenuity into everyday sorcery: toys that walk, lanterns that hum, and ideas that refuse to break.

Culture & Society

Gnome society runs like a beloved workshop: curious, precise, and fair. Virtue means curiosity yoked to craft—measure twice, cite your sources, test before trade, honor patents and apprenticeships—and fraud in tools or weights is both crime and disgrace. Their making is precision with personality: clockworks that purr, hair-true lenses, inks and resins mixed like alchemy, and toys whose whimsy hides serious engineering. Maker’s marks, warranty oaths, and a sacred right-to-repair keep designs honest; once a device is proven safe, diagrams circulate. Trade hums through tinkers’ markets and traveling factors who barter prototypes, lease patents, and ink maintenance contracts. Exports: instruments, springlocks, surveying kits, automata novelties, calibrated lenses. Imports: rare alloys, crystal, volatile oils, fine hardwoods. Salvage rights are codified; scrap sings again after one more pass on the bench.

Families are affectionate hives built around shared projects—grandparents, parents, apprentices, and foster-kin crowd the same benches, passing tools and stories alike. Marriages are partnerships of invention as much as romance, often sealed with co-designed pieces; promising apprentices are readily “adopted,” and elders maintain meticulous ledgers of designs, failures, and lessons so each generation starts farther along the blueprint. Work becomes ceremony: newborns receive a tiny Naming Gear, youths earn their First Fix, and marriages trade Twin Keys in a locking rite that symbolizes trust, access, and mutual responsibility.

Faith & Myth

Gnome faith is practical and warmly irreverent: they lean toward Herukan (maker’s fire, bold invention) and sometimes Ioun (patient knowledge), but their oldest stories insist that craft itself is the true patron—the stubborn spark in gears, glass, and ink that taught small folk after Starfall to measure, mend, and build a future from salvage. They say they “woke to the sound of making,” following a hidden ticking through wreckage until order answered their hands.

Rites are small, binding craft to kin: the Naming Gear for newborns, a youth’s First Fix, marriages sealed by Twin Keys and a Calibration Vigil, and the Remembrance of Hands for the honored dead. Taboos are plain and policed: never sell untested prototypes, never falsify measures or maker’s marks, don’t open another’s device without Bench-Right, and waste nothing that can be safely salvaged. Festivals are bright and useful: Tinker’s Moon with lantern parades and clockwork pageants, and Founders’ Day when benches open for public lessons and free repairs—piety expressed as tools that work and promises kept.

Subspecies

Gnome folk tend to three broad lineages: forest gnomes, soft-voiced whisperers with a knack for small illusions and woodland bonds; rock gnomes, tireless tinkerers and keepers of clever clockworks; and deep gnomes (svirfneblin), flint-hard Underworld survivors famed for stealth, stone-sense, and grim practicality.

Forest Gnomes

Forest gnomes are soft-footed whisperers of the green—small, moss-toned folk who weave harmless illusions like firefly lights and speak to squirrels and sparrows as neighbors. They keep to root-homes and hidden ladders in the boughs, trading in herbs, inks, and clever camouflages rather than steel.Forest

Rock Gnomes

Rock gnomes are tireless bench-dwellers with magnifiers on cords and pockets full of spare springs—masters of fine instruments, sturdy clockworks, and inks that bite true. They prize repeatable design and cheerful iteration, trading calibrated lenses, survey kits, and clever automata that look like toys until they save a life.Rock

Deep gnomes

Deep gnomes (svirfneblin) are flint-hard Underworld survivors—ashen-skinned, soft-voiced, and near-invisible when they choose—whose stone-sense and stoic pragmatism keep them alive where light is a rumor. They trade in cut gems, salts, and precise stonecraft, trusting clever camouflage and quiet routes over banners or boasts.svirfneblin
