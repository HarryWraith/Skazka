Gnomes

Small hands. Vast designs.

Profile

Average Lifespan ~400 years
Typical Size Medium; 3 - 4ft
Homelands Gnomes have no place they call home. Instead choosing to integrate into other cultures.
Languages Trade-tongue, Gnomish
Faiths Gnomes tend not to be overly religious, though when they are they call Herukan their patron
Temperament Inquisitive, meticulous, irrepressible

Overview

The Gnomes of Skazka are a people wrapped in mystery, their origins obscured in the mists of history. Unlike Elves, whose ancestry stretches clearly back to ancient lineages, or Dwarves, who can trace their roots to divine design, Gnomes seem to have simply appeared — a quiet people without fanfare, mythic event, or cosmic purpose. This lack of a definitive origin has become part of their identity, shaping them into a race defined less by destiny and more by ingenuity, adaptability, and a relentless drive to improve the world around them. Where others rely on tradition, brute strength, or divine blessing, Gnomes excel through innovation, subtle progress, and the quiet revolution of everyday invention.

At the heart of every Gnome lies an insatiable curiosity and an unshakable desire to solve problems. To them, the world is not something to be endured, but a puzzle waiting for solutions. Their smiths design weapons and tools that adapt to conditions, their alchemists brew concoctions that make life safer and easier, and their engineers devise mechanisms that save lives in mines or make simple tasks more efficient. Gnomes are not driven by conquest, wealth, or glory—though these may come as side effects—but by the joy of discovery and the satisfaction of creating something that improves the lives of others. They embody the belief that progress need not come from sacrifice, but from dedication, creativity, and the clever application of knowledge.

Gnomes fit seamlessly into the wider fabric of Skazkan society, though always in their own distinctive way. Their underground communities are masterworks of space management and clever engineering, thriving around natural resources and built with astonishing efficiency. Surface settlements, meanwhile, tend to appear in neglected corners of larger towns—areas too cramped or inconvenient for others, which Gnomes transform into vibrant districts filled with workshops, markets, and ingenious innovations. Their culture, unlike many others, values merit above bloodline, with guilds and workshops granting status based on skill and contribution rather than birthright. This meritocracy produces brilliant merchants, teachers, and craftsmen who spread their creations far and wide. Though peaceful by inclination, Gnomes are not defenseless; when threatened, their ingenuity turns to strategy, traps, and tactical warfare. In both war and peace, they prove that progress and survival alike rest on the same foundation—intelligence, creativity, and adaptability.

Physiology & Traits

  • Appearance: Gnomes are small, sharp-eyed, and quick-fingered, with warm earth-toned skin, lively features, wiry hair (often with whiskers), and layered, pocketed clothes stuffed with tools and trinkets.
  • Dimorphism: In gnomes, sex differences are clear to the eye: males are a little taller with squarer shoulders, denser body and facial hair, heavier brows, and deeper voices; females are shorter on average with softer features, fuller hips, finer body hair, and lighter voices. The contrast reads in silhouette—broad-torsoed, bearded tinkerers beside round-hipped, sharp-eyed craftswomen—yet both remain compact and quick-handed by nature.
  • Senses: Gnomes read the world in fine print: dark and dim light bloom for them in soft greys, and they catch the tiny sounds—gear teeth kissing, a latch settling—that others miss. Their noses sort oils, metal filings, and singed varnish at a whiff, making a workshop speak like a book.
  • Innate Gifts: Gnomes are born with a mind that magic can’t easily fool—their wits slip past charms and glamours as if through a hidden service door. Many carry a flicker of knack from their bloodline: forest gnomes idly weave whispers and light into harmless illusions and trade secrets with squirrels and sparrows, while rock gnomes “remember” how things work—names of compounds, quirks of old mechanisms—and can coax clever little clockworks to life from springs, wire, and patience. Small of stature but tireless of curiosity, they turn ingenuity into a kind of everyday sorcery: toys that walk, lanterns that hum, and ideas that refuse to break.

Culture & Society

  • Virtues & Laws: Gnomes prize curiosity yoked to craft: precision, fair dealing, and sharing useful discoveries once the work is proven safe. Their laws read like workshop rules—measure twice, mark your sources, honor patents and apprenticeships, test before trade—and fraud in tools or weights is treated as both a crime and a disgrace.
  • Craft & Trade: Gnome craft is precision with personality: clockworks that purr, lenses true to the hair’s breadth, inks and resins mixed like alchemy, and toys whose whimsy hides serious engineering. Workshops run on apprenticeships, maker’s marks, and warranty oaths; every tool has a provenance, and “right-to-repair” is sacred—diagrams are shared once a design is proven safe. Trade favors bustling tinkers’ markets and traveling factors who barter prototypes, lease patents, and arrange maintenance contracts. Exports include fine instruments, springlocks, surveying kits, automata novelties, and calibrated lenses; imports trend toward rare alloys, crystal, volatile oils, and hardwoods. Nothing is wasted: salvage rights are codified, and scrap returns to the bench until it sings.
  • Family & Names: Gnome families are busy, affectionate hives built around workshops and shared projects: grandparents, parents, apprentices, and foster-kin crowd the same benches, passing tools and stories in equal measure. Marriages are partnerships of invention as much as romance—vows often sealed by co-designed pieces—and children grow amid safe, supervised tinkering, earning their first toolroll when they can maintain it without prompting. Kinship stretches by mentorship and patent-sharing as readily as blood; it’s common to “adopt” a promising apprentice into the household, and elders keep meticulous family ledgers of designs, failures, and lessons so each generation starts a little further along the blueprint.
  • Customs & Rites: Gnome customs turn work into ceremony:
    • newborns receive a tiny Naming Gear
    • Youths prove themselves with a First Fix
    • marriages are sealed by Twin Keys — each forges a key which are exchanged during the locking ceremony

Faith & Myth

  • Patrons: Gnomes lean towards Herukan but sometimes Ioun
  • Origin Story: Gnomes say they “woke to the sound of making”—the first to rise after Starfall were small folk who followed a steady, hidden ticking through the wreckage and learned to coax order from broken things. In their oldest tales, craft itself is their patron: not a single god but the stubborn spark in gears, glass, and ink that taught them to measure, mend, and build a future from salvage.
  • Rites & Taboos: Gnome rites center on the Naming Gear, First Fix, Twin Keys with a Calibration Vigil, and the Remembrance of Hands—small ceremonies that bind craft to kin. Their taboos are plain: never sell untested prototypes, never falsify measures or maker’s marks, don’t open another’s device without Bench-Right, and waste nothing that can be safely salvaged.
  • Omens & Festivals (Sun Elves):Gnome festivals are bright, practical feasts:
    • Tinker’s Moon with lantern parades and clockwork pageants
    • Founders’ Day when workshops open their benches for public lessons and free repairs

Subspecies

Gnome folk tend to three broad lineages: forest gnomes, soft-voiced whisperers with a knack for small illusions and woodland bonds; rock gnomes, tireless tinkerers and keepers of clever clockworks; and deep gnomes (svirfneblin), flint-hard Underworld survivors famed for stealth, stone-sense, and grim practicality.

Forest Gnomes

Forest gnomes are soft-footed whisperers of the green—small, moss-toned folk who weave harmless illusions like firefly lights and speak to squirrels and sparrows as neighbors. They keep to root-homes and hidden ladders in the boughs, trading in herbs, inks, and clever camouflages rather than steel.Forest

Rock Gnomes

Rock gnomes are tireless bench-dwellers with magnifiers on cords and pockets full of spare springs—masters of fine instruments, sturdy clockworks, and inks that bite true. They prize repeatable design and cheerful iteration, trading calibrated lenses, survey kits, and clever automata that look like toys until they save a life.Rock

Deep gnomes

Deep gnomes (svirfneblin) are flint-hard Underworld survivors—ashen-skinned, soft-voiced, and near-invisible when they choose—whose stone-sense and stoic pragmatism keep them alive where light is a rumor. They trade in cut gems, salts, and precise stonecraft, trusting clever camouflage and quiet routes over banners or boasts.svirfneblin
