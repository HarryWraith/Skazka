A lone adventurer steps off the longboat that has taken him to the necropolis of Dargav. The aging jetty creaks underfoot and the forest seems to mimic the sound. A crow lands on a nearby branch to judge the visitor. It caws, perhaps in approval? Clutching the urn he has brought here, the warrior takes a deep breath and steps forward. This place is hallowed so he must tread carefully.Back where he began his journey, the streets of Calarium are choked with the dust from passing horses and carts. A small child weaves through a crowd of merchants and soft skinned gentry. Spotting her mark, she approaches confidently. With practiced ease she feigns a trip and bumps into the unsuspecting gem cutter. With an apology she pushes away from the man, but not empty handed. The concealed blade in one hand has liberated a purse of its strings. Stepping casually away from the crowd she slips into a darkened alleyway to assess her work. Only five gold coins. Not bad, but not enough to get her to Ku'Than. That will have to wait, for now.A three week journey away her sister stifles in the humidity of Ku'Than's forested canopy. Insects constantly gnaw at her ankles and the sweat stings her eyes. Her companion, a small white owl, hoots impatiently. She bends down to retrieve the bottle that is the focus of her task. Inside, a tiny winged creature flutters and bashes the glass in frustration, its lips mouthing silent curses. The Witchling wonders what will become of the fairy; her mistress and mentor is oft cruel. "I won't be like that when I'm a full witch", she tells herself.

Skazka is a vast world where roughly 60% of the surface is covered by water or ice. There are two continents separated by a vast ocean filled with danger, mystery and unexplored islands. Skia, the western continent's location has been lost to all but the collective memory of the Sken, who traversed the great ocean to reach Snuuflund far to the east. The Eastern continent, Kelos, is populated by the races of more than a dozen peoples. Goblins, Kobolds, Elves, Dwarves, and Beast Folk, all call Kelos home.

Western continent: Skia

Little is known of the western continent except what is retold in the oral traditions of the Sken. Skia was once home to men who were servants to the giants; the children of the old Gods. But the Gods abandoned their children and the giants turned to infighting. As man grew in number the giants were no longer able to hold them in servitude. The giants headed north to the mountains and took refuge in the vast fortresses of ice.Man learned to tend crops, herd cattle and mine the earth. They were quick learners and soon held mastery over their domain. But they were not satisfied with their lot and turned to exploration. Further, higher and deeper, they went. Their knowledge grew and the Gods took note. They could not allow man to become too knowledgeable lest they seek godhood themselves.The Gods argued bitterly on what should be done. But ultimately they chose to do nothing, thinking that man's brazeness would be his undoing. These stories are now the stuff of legend: half truth and half myth. What is beyond refute is that man awoke something perhaps as old as the Gods. Something so terrible that they abandoned their homes, farms and mines, and fled east in search of a land that was just a myth to them.

Eastern continent: Kelos

The continent of Kelos sits between the equator and the north pole of the planet. Consequently, this vast continent has many biomes. From the harsh glaciers of the far north to the parched desert at the most southerly tip of the continent, temperatures range from one extreme to the other.As much variety can also be seen again in the inhabitants of Kelos. From the ancient Dwarves that once served as Smiths to the Gods to the Orc tribes created for war, the races of Kelos have a rich and colourful history that spans thousands of years.

The Serydina Isles

These are a small chain of islands a days voyage off the west coast of Kelos. Snuuflund is the largest of these isles, but there are several others that are inhabited as well as number of smaller islands only frequented by sheep and other species of animal.

Oy: the mythical island of the gods

Oy is the mythical home of the first Gods. Its existence is a hotly debated subject among both theolurgens and archeologists. Some argue that Oy physically exists on Skazka while others believe that it is not on Skazka, but rather connected to it in some way.
