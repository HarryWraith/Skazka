In the shadowed realm of Skazka, the line between hero and villain blurs like mist in the moonlight. Here, where ancient powers stir beneath cursed skies and forgotten magics pulse through the very earth, those who dare to carve their names into legend are rarely what they first appear to be. The champions of this realm are forged not in glory, but in darkness—tempered by trials that would break lesser souls and shaped by choices that demand terrible prices.

Within these pages, you will encounter the souls who have left their mark upon Skazka's blood-soaked history. From the noble fallen and the righteously wrathful to the cunningly beautiful and the unspeakably ancient, each character represents a thread in the greater web of this realm's dark mythology. They are the puppet masters and the pawns, the saviors and the damned, the whispered names that make even the bravest adventurers pause before speaking certain words aloud.

Remember, traveler, that in a land where every legend is born from truth and every truth carries the weight of legend, these are not mere names to be catalogued, but living forces that continue to shape the very fabric of reality. Choose your allies carefully, for in Skazka, today's hero may be tomorrow's greatest nightmare.

King Theldon - the wild boar

The wild boar, as he is known, became King of Nav'golam upon his father's death 658. He is best known for locking the gates to the city at the start of the Shadow War in the year 700 and refusing to allow admitance to outsiders for 101 years.

Ilistran Darkweaver

Ilistran Darkweaver rose to prominence as the enigmatic leader of the Order of the Word, one of Skazka's most powerful mage guilds. A master of chaos magic, Darkweaver possessed an insatiable hunger for power that extended far beyond the traditional bounds of arcane study. Unlike his peers who viewed magic as a tool for knowledge and protection, Ilistran believed that those who wielded such extraordinary abilities had not only the right but the duty to rule over the mundane world. His charismatic leadership and formidable magical prowess initially drew many followers to his cause, as he promised a new age where mages would emerge from the shadows to claim their rightful place as the world's masters.

However, Darkweaver's radical vision ultimately fractured the very guild he led. When other members of the Order of the Word recognized the dangerous extremes of his ambition, they rose in rebellion against their former leader, sparking the devastating conflict known as the Shadow War. Desperate to maintain his grip on power and crush the insurgency, Ilistran turned to forbidden practices, seeking allies from the treacherous astral plane. His reckless manipulation of chaos magic tore holes in the fabric of reality itself, creating the phenomenon known as The Veil—unstable rifts that connected Skazka to other worlds and dimensions.

The legacy of Ilistran Darkweaver remains one of ambition corrupted by power and the catastrophic consequences of unchecked magical experimentation. The tears he created in reality continue to threaten the stability of Skazka, serving as a permanent reminder of how even the most gifted mages can become the architects of their world's destruction when they allow their desire for dominance to eclipse their wisdom and restraint.

The Stranger

The Stranger is a mysterious individual who has appeared frequently throughout the history of Skazka.

In addition to eye witness testimony, he has been captured in both paintings and literature. He is viewed as an angel of prophecy by some, allegedly having appeared at the site and time of various historical events of note. He is seen as benevolent by some but a sign of impending tragedy by others.

Irrespective of lore and supersticion, he always appears dressed the same: in immauclately clean white suits and carrying a notebook and quil. It is said that he appears and disappears in the blink of an eye.

Maldrath the fire witch

Maldrath was an enigmatic sorceress whose arrival in the north forever altered the tapestry of Winter’s fate. A wanderer from the verdant south, she brought with her not only flame and arcane power but also an intricate charisma that could orchestrate loyalty from even the most hardened hunters. She offered promises of victuals, riches, and glory, and in doing so convinced four score adventurers to embark on the doomed expedition against the dragon Icethorn.

To her followers, she was a captivating figure, weaving a mosaic of power and possibility that seemed to transcend the frozen world’s despair. To her enemies, she was a labyrinth of ambition and deceit, a firebrand who courted catastrophe. She led her hunters across the River Horn, where triumph soon curdled into tragedy: the death of Glaclalis, Icethorn’s mate, and the curse that fell upon Grimspire.

Though her ultimate fate is uncertain, legends intertwine with whispers that Maldrath’s spirit lingers still, a burning shade in the frozen halls, her fire forever trapped in ice. For some, she is a cautionary tale; for others, an invitation to delve into ambition’s crucible, where danger and desire beckon in equal measure.

"She came like summer fire to our frozen lives, all promises of gold and glory… and left nothing but ashes. I still dream of her laughter, carried on the wind, and wonder how we ever followed a witch into the teeth of a dragon."

King Alaric IV

The present sovereign of Caratania, King Alaric IV, stands as both a living echo of his forebears and a mystery unto himself. Now in his mid-forties, Alaric is often remarked upon for his striking resemblance to the monarchs of old—so much so that courtiers and common folk alike whisper that his likeness could have stepped directly from the royal portraits that line the halls of the capital.

Though rarely seen beyond the palace walls, those few who have encountered him speak of an uncanny swiftness that defies the natural measure of men. It is said he moves with the speed of a hawk in flight, though such claims blur the line between truth and legend.

Deeply devout, Alaric is known as one of the most ardent followers of Aeter in Caratania’s history. His rule is guided less by earthly counsel than by the teachings of the Eternal Light, to whom he is said to pray for many hours each day. This devotion has only deepened during his long seclusion.

For four years the King has not appeared in public, and the people of Caratania speak his name with a blend of reverence and unease. Is he communing more closely with Aeter than ever before, or has some secret malady, physical or spiritual, claimed him from their sight?

Yet the royal banners still fly, and his seal still marks the decrees of governance. Whether glimpsed or hidden, King Alaric IV remains the heart of Caratania—unchanged in visage, quick as a shadow, and faithful beyond all measure.
